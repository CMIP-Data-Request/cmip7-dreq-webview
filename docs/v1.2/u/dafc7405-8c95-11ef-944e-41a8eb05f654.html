Category Description

These are non-exclusive grouping of CMIP variables (e.g. monthly time slices of the baseline variables). Variables can belong to more than one group.



AttributeValue
justificationThe proposed set of monthly variables are considered high priority for DAMIP to assess changes in the climate system and the role of forcings in driving those changes. - Basic fields for assessing global mean temperature, hydrological, sea level, and circulation changes: tas, ts, tos, pr, ps, prsn, psl, mrsos, mrros, tauu, tauv, evspsbl, mrro, uas, vas, huss, hurs, ta, ua, va, wap, zos, zostoga, siconc, siconca, sithick, snw, sfcWind - Fluxes for diagnosing surface and top of atmosphere energy balances: hfls, hflss, rlds, rlus, rsds, rsus, rsdt, rsut, rtmt - Quantities for understanding the role of clouds: rsdsc, rsusc, clt, clwvi, clivi - Quantities for diagnosing the change in ocean circulation e.g., examples of the use of DAMIP in analyzing ocean circulation behavior is Menary et al (2020) 10.1029/2020GL088166 and Simpson et al (2023) 10.1175/JCLI-D-22-0666.s1 : hfbasin, msftmrho, msftmz, so, thetao, uo, vo - Quantities for understanding differences in co2 concentrations from emissions driven simulations: co2, fgco2, nbp - Quantities for diagnosing the various forcings that the climate system is being influenced by: loadso4, abs550aer, abs550bc, abs550so4, od550aer, od550soa, od550dust, od550volso4, o3ste, tropo3ste - Zonal mean atmospheric temperature at high vertical resolution (AERmonZ.ta) but 3D would also work. There's a desire to have atmospheric temperature with higher vertical resolution at upper levels to better capturing stratospheric satellite weighting functions.
